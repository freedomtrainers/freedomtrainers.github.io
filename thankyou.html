Election: Our Civic Holiday

Thanks for signing up!

You're in — and that matters. You've committed to treating November 3rd as a Civic Holiday: a day you give to your community, your neighbors, and your right to have a say in the decisions that affect your life.

"In America, voters are supposed to pick our leaders. Let's act like it."

Now, three things to make it real:

Step 1
Block off November 3rd right now. Put it in your calendar. Set a reminder. Tell your employer you're using it for civic participation — many are legally required to give you time to vote, and more are offering a full day. The day only works if you protect the time.
Step 2
Find your thing to do — if you aren't sure, find something at Ground Control → Whether you want to be a poll worker, drive voters to the polls, serve as an election observer, or organize a welcoming presence near your polling place — Ground Control connects you to the specific opportunity that fits your day. Don't just show up. Show up with a role.
Step 3
Tell three people. That's it. Three friends, family members, or coworkers who should be spending November 3rd the same way. Text them. Forward this email. Post about it. Every person you bring in multiplies what we can do together.

Also Worth Knowing

Check your voter registration at vote.org — and tell your three people to check theirs. Voter rolls are being purged. Checking once isn't enough; check again 30 days before the election.

If you want to vote early (and we encourage it), Vote Early Day is October 27, 2026. Register your group early voting event at voteearlyday.org. Voting together is a statement as much as an act.

Spread the Word

The more people who commit to this, the harder it becomes to keep anyone from voting.
